Fighter Jets

The Forth Generation

A New Era

The fourth generation of fighters is about superior manoeuvrability, advanced avionics, and multi-role capabilities. They feature high power-to-weight ratios, making them able to better dogfight and intercept. Many aircraft fit into this category, such as the F-15(pictured here), the F-16, Su-27, and Su 30. These airframes were so well designed and adaptable that many of them are still used today in upgraded forms known as 4.5 gen fighters.

The Typhoon

The Eurofighter Typhoon is a highly agile 4.5-gen fighter built around manoeuvrability, advanced avionics, and multi-role performance. Its lightweight airframe and powerful engines give it an exceptional power-to-weight ratio, making it excel in both dogfighting and interception. Designed by a group of European nations, the Typhoon combines modern sensors with the agility of earlier fourth-gen jets, making it a very capable non-stealth fighter.

The Flanker Family

The Russian Su-27 “Flanker” is known for its powerful engines, high manoeuvrability, and large, versatile airframe, making it one of the most adaptable fighter designs of its era. Because of its adaptability, it was evolved into numerous variants, including the Su-30, Su-33, Su-34, and Su-35. Each adaptation has new avionics, sensors, and systems, making the basic design into either a carrier-capable fighter or a heavy strike aircraft. This flexibility is why the Flanker family of jets is still widely used today and continues to be upgraded into modern forms.

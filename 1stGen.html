Fighter Jets

The WW2 Era

The First Fighter Jet

The first ever fighter jet to fly was the He-178, an experimental German fighter. On August 27, 1939, it took off and circled it's factory using only the power of it's jet engine. Although this plane was the first to fly, it was never used in combat. This is because German officials preferred the more powerful and advanced Me-262. This jet, as shown to the left, was the first operational jet fighter.

Britain's Response

Although the Germans beat the British to putting a jet in the sky, they weren't far behind, and they soon developed the Gloster Meteor(shown in the heading image). This proved to be a powerful adversary to the German Messerschmitt. Later in the war, the second British fighter entered service. It was the deHavilland Vampire, which as shown to the right, featured a distinct twin-boom tail configuration. The Vampire was highly maneuverable, and also pioneered single-engine designs.

Fighter Jets

The History of Fighter Jets

Where we are today

Today our skies are home to some of humanity's most advanced machines. capable of stealth, speed, and agility to outperform and destroy their adversaries. They feature advanced radar, extensive computer systems, and long range missiles. Many of these planes use radar absorbing paint and geometry to hide them from enemy radar. They are at the cutting edge of technology, and seem more like spacecraft than planes.

Where it All Began

The first fighter jet took flight on August 27, 1939. The experimental He 178 circled the factory only using the power of its two jet engines. This plane was never used in combat though, so the first operational jet fighter was actually the German Me-262. Soon after the Me-262 was put into service, Britain answered with its first jet, the Gloster Meteor.

The Forth Generation

In the 1970s and 80s a forth generation of fighters emerged. They featured advanced maneuverability, fly-by-wire flight control systems, and digital avionics. Many of these jets were upgraded into “4.5 Gen” fighters, with better avionics and extended lifetime.

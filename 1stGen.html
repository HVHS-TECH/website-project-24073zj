Fighter Jets

The WW2 Era

The First Fighter Jet

The first ever fighter jet to fly was the He-178, an experimental German fighter. On August 27, 1939, the jet took off and circled its factory using only the power of its jet engine. Although this plane was the first to fly, it was never used in combat. This is because German officials preferred the more powerful and advanced Me-262. This jet, as shown to the left, was the first jet powered fighter to see combat.

Britain's Response

Although the Germans were first to put a jet in the sky, the British weren't far behind, and they soon developed the Gloster Meteor. This proved to be a powerful adversary to the German jet. Later in the war, the second British fighter entered service. It was the deHavilland Vampire, which as shown to the right, featured a distinct twin-boom tail configuration. The Vampire was highly maneuverable, and also pioneered single-engine designs.

America's First Jet

Although the US is home to some of the most advanced fighters in the world today, they actually developed their first jet a bit later than Germany and the UK. They made the P-80 Shooting Star, developed in secret at Lockheed Martin and capable of blistering speeds. It quickly set a world speed record in 1947 of 624 mph. Although it was not seen in WW2, it was soon used in the Korean war.

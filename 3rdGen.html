Fighter Jets

The Multi-Role Era

A Shift in Focus

The third generation is when fighter jets started to transition from single purpose aircraft, like interceptors and air superiority jets, to multi role fighters. Most jets in this era were designed to be versatile and customizable, so the same fighter could do bombing missions or dogfighting. A notable fighter in this era was the Dassault Mirage III, because it was designed to be changed and modified for different purposes. Its simple airframe was very flexible, allowing for multiple variants and weapon loadouts.

The F-4 Phantom

America's most notable third gen fighter was the F-4 Phantom II. It was a major player in the Vietnam war, and shows the multi role traits typical of this era. The Phantom was a two-seat interceptor and fighter bomber that could take off from aircraft carriers. It was a great fighter for air superiority and attack until it was replaced by the FA-18 Hornet.

The Fishbed

One of the Soviet Union's most successful third generation fighters was the Mig-21(NATO name Fishbed). It was a delta-winged single engine jet capable of Mach 2 and its basic configuration was re-used many times on other jets. The Mig was a successful fighter-interceptor that had a very fast rate of climb and could nearly equal the F-16's qualities in dogfight. It was the most produced fighter after the Mig-15, showing its success on the export market.

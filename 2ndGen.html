Fighter Jets

The Second Generation

The First Second Gen Fighter

As the Korean war was heating up, both sides were racing to put better and faster jets in the sky. The first fighter of the second generation was the Mig-15, surprising the United States with its speed and agility. This aircraft was so successful that it became one of the most produced jet aircraft with more than 13,000 manufactured in total.

The Sabre

The American fighters were being battered by the Mig-15's. Their superior speed and turning rate made them powerful enemies. To counter this, the US created the F-86 Sabre, their first jet with swept wings. It had a powerful engine and high max speeds, and this greatly helped them in the skies over Korea.

Britain's Second Gen Fighter

The Hawker Hunter was a manoeuvrable interceptor jet developed in the UK. It first flew on 20 July 1951, and turned out to be a very successful jet. It included a swept wing for improved aerodynamics, and the powerful Avon engine. The Hunter saw service in multiple wars until it was replaced by the Lightning.
